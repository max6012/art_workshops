Portrait Drawing & Painting Workshop

February 15 & 22, 2026

We are looking forward to drawing and painting with you in our upcoming two-day portrait workshop! We'll explore how to see the face as shapes rather than features, how to capture likeness through proportion and value, and how to translate those foundations into a painted portrait.

Our focus will be on learning, not perfection — think of your drawings and paintings as experiments, not masterpieces.

We'll start each day with demos and exercises to loosen up, then move into longer sessions where you'll apply what you've learned. By the end, you'll have a better understanding of how to see, measure, and render the human face like an artist.

Workshop Schedule

Day 1: Foundations in Pencil & Charcoal — Sunday, February 15

9:00 – 9:30 Welcome & Setup

Intro to materials, safety, and workshop philosophy: process over product, "disposable drawings," experimentation.

9:30 – 9:45 Seeing Shapes, Not Features

Quick discussion of why our brains lie to us about faces — how we see "eyes" and "noses" instead of shapes, angles, and values.

9:45 – 10:30 Pencil Warm-Ups

Gesture drawings of the head — 2-minute sketches focusing on overall shape and tilt, not features. We're training our eyes, not making keepers.

10:30 – 11:00 Demo: Proportion & Measurement

Instructor demo covering comparative measurement, the classic proportion guidelines (and when to ignore them), mapping big shapes.

11:00 – 12:00 Pencil Studies

Students complete 3–4 pencil studies (10–15 minutes each) focusing on head shape, placement of features as shapes, and proportion.

12:00 – 12:45 Lunch
12:45 – 1:15 Introduction to Charcoal

Demo of vine vs. compressed charcoal, subtractive drawing, building value.

1:15 – 2:30 Charcoal Studies

Students complete 2–3 charcoal studies (15–20 minutes each) focusing on big value shapes and shadow patterns, resisting the urge to draw features too soon.

2:30 – 3:00 Group Review & Discussion

Discussion of what worked: shape accuracy vs. feature rendering, value grouping, we're learning to see the abstraction underneath the face.

Day 2: From Charcoal to Color — Sunday, February 22

9:00 – 9:30 Warm-Up: Quick Charcoal Study

One charcoal portrait to loosen up and reinforce yesterday's lessons. Focus on value structure — this becomes your roadmap for painting.

9:30 – 9:45 From Drawing to Painting

Brief discussion of how drawing skills translate to paint: value structure first, color second.

9:45 – 10:00 Demo: Monochromatic Block-In

Instructor demo of blocking in values with a single color — establishing the structure before introducing full color.

10:00 – 11:00 Monochromatic Block-In Studies

Students complete 1–2 value block-in studies, focusing on getting the drawing right in paint.

11:00 – 11:15 Demo: Adding Color

Instructor demo of transitioning from value block-in to color — mixing flesh tones, maintaining value relationships.

11:15 – 12:00 Color Studies

Students add color to their block-ins or start fresh, focusing on matching values while introducing warm/cool temperature shifts.

12:00 – 12:45 Lunch
12:45 – 2:30 The "Good Enough" Portrait

Students develop one portrait painting from value block-in through color to "good enough," emphasizing not overworking it — knowing when to stop.

2:30 – 3:00 Group Reflection

Group discussion and reflection on the weekend's progression from shapes to values to color.

Materials Provided

Newsprint, drawing paper, graphite pencils, vine and compressed charcoal, kneaded erasers, canvas paper, limited palette oils, brushes, solvent, rags.

What to Bring

  • Your own lunch and plenty of water
  • An apron or clothes you don't mind getting dirty
  • A roll of paper towels
  • A portrait reference photo — ideally a printed image with clear lighting, but you can also use one on your iPhone or iPad
