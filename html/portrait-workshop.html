Portrait Painting Workshop

February 15 & 22, 2026

Join us for a two-day portrait painting workshop held over two consecutive Sundays. We will explore the application of color theory to skin tones, practice capturing likeness with form relationships and values, and finish with a color oil sketch. Day one will focus on theory and drawing, and day two on color. We will be drawing and painting from life. All levels are welcome!

Example Works

Workshop Schedule

Day 1 — Sunday, February 15

Focus: Theory & Drawing

9:00 – 9:30 Welcome & Setup — Introduction to materials and workshop philosophy
9:30 – 10:30 Anatomy of the Head — Proportions, planes, and landmarks of the face
10:30 – 12:00 Drawing from Life — Gesture and construction drawings from live model
12:00 – 12:45 Lunch Break
12:45 – 2:15 Value Studies — Capturing form through light and shadow
2:15 – 3:00 Group Review & Discussion

Day 2 — Sunday, February 22

Focus: Color

9:00 – 9:30 Warm-up & Review
9:30 – 10:30 Color Theory for Skin Tones — Warm/cool relationships, color temperature in flesh
10:30 – 12:00 Mixing Practice — Creating a skin tone palette from limited colors
12:00 – 12:45 Lunch Break
12:45 – 2:30 Color Oil Sketch — Painting from life, applying color theory to portrait
2:30 – 3:00 Group Reflection & Critique

What to Bring

  • Your own lunch and plenty of water
  • An apron or clothes you don't mind getting paint on
  • A roll of paper towels

Drawing and painting supplies and live model included.

Workshop Cost: $200 for both days
