Seascape Painting Workshop

Saturday, March 7, 2026

We are looking forward to painting with you in this one-day seascape workshop! We'll explore how master painters have captured the sea across centuries, then apply those techniques to bring out the vibrancy of St. Thomas seascapes in your own paintings.

Our focus will be on learning, not perfection — think of your paintings as experiments, not masterpieces.

We'll start by looking at how the greats handled water, light, and atmosphere, move into quick studies from provided references, then spend the afternoon developing a painting from your own reference photo.

All materials will be provided, but feel free to bring your favorite brushes and paints. All levels are welcome.

Instructor's Works

Workshop Schedule

Saturday, March 7

9:00 – 9:30 Welcome & Setup

Intro to materials, safety, and workshop philosophy: process over product, experimentation over perfection.

9:30 – 10:15 Learning from the Masters

We'll look at seascapes from the Baroque through contemporary painters — how they handled water movement, light on waves, atmospheric perspective, and the challenge of making blue interesting.

10:15 – 12:00 Quick Studies from Reference

Students complete several color sketches (20–30 minutes each) from provided reference images, focusing on value structure, color temperature shifts in water, and not overworking the waves.

12:00 – 12:45 Lunch
12:45 – 1:00 Choosing & Simplifying Your Reference

Brief discussion of what makes a good seascape reference — clear light, simple composition, interesting shapes. How to crop and simplify.

1:00 – 2:30 Your Seascape

Students develop a painting from their own reference photo, applying the morning's lessons. Instructor circulates for individual guidance.

2:30 – 3:00 Group Reflection

Group discussion of the day's work — what techniques clicked, what you'll take into your next seascape.

What to Bring

  • Your own lunch and plenty of water
  • An apron or clothes you don't mind getting paint on
  • A roll of paper towels
  • A seascape reference photo — ideally a printed image with clear light and simple composition, but you can also use one on your iPhone or iPad

All materials will be provided, but feel free to bring your favorite brushes and paints.
