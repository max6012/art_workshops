I Paint because cameras flatten time into frozen slices. A painting can hold what's retained, what's immediate, and what's anticipated all at once — not capturing a moment, but reflecting how we inhabit it.

Light on water shows this. The human face demands it — the face of the Other that can't be totalized, that overflows any attempt to grasp it, that demands presence and response. A portrait isn't depicting someone. It's an encounter. I invite you to explore mine.

I'm currently the artist-in-residence at Island Art Studio in St. Thomas, where I paint, teach, and try to impart this way of seeing — or rather, this way of being present. The studio hosts workshops in portrait drawing, painting, and seascape techniques throughout the year.
