The Seascape Through the Ages

$25

Thursday, February 27, 2026 - 6:00 PM

From the dramatic seas of the Dutch Golden Age to the luminous waters of the Impressionists, this lecture traces how artists have captured the ocean's ever-changing nature. We'll examine techniques for depicting water movement, light on waves, and atmospheric perspective - skills directly applicable to your own seascape paintings.

Topics Covered

  • Dutch marine painting and the birth of seascape as a genre
  • Turner's revolutionary approach to light and atmosphere
  • Winslow Homer and the American seascape tradition
  • Impressionist techniques for capturing fleeting water effects
  • Contemporary approaches and what we can learn from them
Duration: 90 minutes Location: Studio Gallery, St. Thomas Includes: Refreshments
Reserve Seat - $25

Seeing Like an Artist: The Science of Observation

$25

Thursday, March 20, 2026 - 6:00 PM

Why do our brains lie to us when we try to draw? This lecture explores the neuroscience behind artistic seeing - why beginners draw symbols instead of what they see, and how trained artists learn to bypass these cognitive shortcuts. Understanding these principles will transform how you approach any subject.

Topics Covered

  • How the brain processes visual information
  • Symbol systems vs. perceptual drawing
  • Techniques masters use to "see" accurately
  • Practical exercises to train your eye
  • Why measuring and comparing beats memory
Duration: 90 minutes Location: Studio Gallery, St. Thomas Includes: Refreshments
Reserve Seat - $25
